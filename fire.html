What is the FIRE Movement?

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It is a philosophy centered on maximizing your savings rate and investing in assets until your portfolio can sustain your life indefinitely.

Lean FIRE

Extreme frugality. The goal is to retire as quickly as possible on a restricted budget (approx. $25k-$40k/year).

Fat FIRE

Retire early without sacrificing luxury. Requires a larger nest egg for annual spending of $100k+.

Coast FIRE

Front-load investments early so you can stop saving and let compound interest do the rest of the work.

The Three Pillars of FIRE

Achieving early retirement isn't about luck; it's a mathematical process involving these three components:

1. Aggressive Savings Rate: The "secret sauce" of FIRE is how much you keep. By saving 50% or more of your income, you reduce the years of work needed to fund each year of retirement.

2. Low-Cost Index Investing: Avoid high fees. Investing in broad market ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds) ensures you capture the growth of the global economy.

3. The 4% Rule: Based on historical data, withdrawing 4% of your starting portfolio annually allows your funds to last indefinitely through market cycles.
