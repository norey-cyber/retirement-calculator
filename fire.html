What is the FIRE Movement?

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It is a philosophy centered on maximizing your savings rate and investing in low-cost assets until your portfolio can sustain your life indefinitely.

Lean FIRE

Extreme frugality and minimalist living. The goal is to retire as quickly as possible on a restricted budget (approx. $25k-$40k/year).

Fat FIRE

Maintaining a high-quality lifestyle without sacrifice. This requires a much larger nest egg to support annual spending of $100k+.

Coast FIRE

Front-loading your investments early so that even if you stop saving today, the portfolio will grow to your target by traditional retirement age.

The Three Pillars of FIRE

1. Aggressive Savings Rate: The "secret sauce" of FIRE isn't how much you earn, but how much you keep. By keeping expenses low and saving 50% or more of your income, you reduce the time it takes to replace your working income with investment returns.

2. Low-Cost Index Investing: Minimize the fees paid to fund managers. Investing in broad market ETFs (like the S&P 500) ensures you capture the growth of the overall economy over time.

3. The 4% Rule (Safe Withdrawal Rate): Based on historical data, withdrawing 4% of your starting retirement portfolio (adjusted for inflation) allows the money to last 30 years or more. Dividing your annual expenses by 0.04 (or multiplying by 25) gives you your target FIRE number.
